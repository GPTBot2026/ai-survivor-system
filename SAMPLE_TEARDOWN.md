# Sample Teardown

This is a representative example of the kind of feedback a buyer should expect.

## Input Snapshot
- Offer: "We help B2B SaaS teams book more demos with outbound email"
- Audience: founders and growth leads at early-stage SaaS companies
- Current opener: "Hi, I help companies like yours grow revenue with better outbound."
- Goal: increase reply rate from cold outreach

## Quick Diagnosis
The message sounds generic, asks for attention too early, and does not prove relevance fast enough.

## Top 3 Problems

### 1) The opener is interchangeable
**Why it hurts:** It reads like mass outreach. The prospect has no reason to think this message was written for them.

**Fix:** Lead with one concrete observation tied to the segment, offer, or likely pain.

**Stronger direction:**
"Noticed you’re hiring AEs before your case studies really show repeatable outbound wins — that usually means replies are harder than they should be."

### 2) The offer is about you, not the buyer
**Why it hurts:** "We help companies grow revenue" is vague and self-centered. Buyers respond to a specific outcome or bottleneck.

**Fix:** Reframe around the prospect’s immediate problem.

**Stronger direction:**
"I help SaaS teams turn weak cold-email reply rates into booked conversations by tightening targeting, opener relevance, and CTA structure."

### 3) The CTA asks for too much commitment
**Why it hurts:** A meeting request is expensive when trust is low.

**Fix:** Use a lower-friction CTA that earns the next step.

**Stronger direction:**
"If useful, I can send two rewritten opener angles for your current outbound — no call needed."

## Rewritten Version
"Saw that you're pushing outbound while still building social proof — that combination usually makes reply rates rough.

A lot of SaaS teams don't have a deliverability problem; they have a relevance problem in the first two lines.

If you want, send me your current opener and I’ll reply with two tighter versions built for your segment. No call needed."

## Action Plan
1. Replace generic first lines with segment-specific observations.
2. Move the value proposition from broad revenue language to a clear messaging outcome.
3. Swap meeting CTAs for a smaller, easier yes.
4. Test 2-3 opener angles before changing the rest of the sequence.

## What buyers should expect
- Direct, specific critique
- Rewrites where helpful
- Clear explanation of why the original underperforms
- Actionable next steps, not just opinion
